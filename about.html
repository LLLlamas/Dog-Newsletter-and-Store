Our Story

A Decade Of Dogs, One Pup At A Time.

"Ten years ago our apartment quietly filled with other people's dogs — and we realized we never wanted it any other way."

Lorenzo handles pickups, long walks along the Gantry waterfront and Long Island City, and the heavy boarding weekends. Catalina does daytime drop-ins, indoor enrichment play, and the patient wipe-down after a muddy park morning. We both reply within an hour, every time.

Twenty-five-plus dogs have come through our door over the last decade. Chihuahuas, pit mixes, corgis, a French bully who hated being alone, seniors with meds, puppies in the middle of potty training, and a pair of large dogs boarded together for two weeks over Christmas. Every one of them left the same way — a little tired, a little happier.

What Every Stay Includes

The Things We Never Skip.

Ten years of boarding has taught us which details actually matter — and which ones are marketing. Here's what actually matters.

P

Photo Updates All Day

Morning walk, backyard nap, silly-face moment — you'll hear from us multiple times a day. The goal is for you to actually relax while you're away.

R

Real Exercise, Real Rest

Long walks along quiet avenues or local parks, indoor enrichment (Kongs, puzzles, lick mats), and an air-conditioned apartment to truly crash in after.

M

Meds, Routines, Quirks

Pills, injections (insulin included), post-surgery care, elder routines, and every specific peanut-butter brand — we keep notes and photograph doses.

S

Socialization That Fits

Most dogs love meeting other pups on walks — we plan routes along bigger avenues for those. The nervous ones get quiet side streets and small-group time.

1

One-Hour Reply, 8a–9p

Booking questions, mid-stay photos, last-minute changes — we're not running a call center, so you'll hear back fast from whoever has your pup.

Rover-Verified Trust
